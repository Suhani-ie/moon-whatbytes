QUESTIONS WE ACTUALLY GET

Consent, privacy, and
the stuff that matters.

We built the consent flow before anything else. Here's exactly how it works.

The recording stops for everyone. Moon doesn't keep a partial recording or a transcript up to the point of decline — if anyone opts out, nothing is saved. The meeting continues normally; Moon just steps back and goes silent.

Every participant in the call — not just the host. This is the thing we built first and the thing we'll never walk back. There's no silent recording mode, no "host-only" visibility setting, no way to hide it. If Moon is in a meeting, everyone in the meeting knows it.

Yes. Any participant — not just the host — can request removal of their contributions from a transcript or recording after the fact. Removal requests are processed within 48 hours. The summary and action items for the meeting are updated to reflect the gap, and the requesting participant is notified when it's done.

No. Your recordings, transcripts, and summaries are never used to train AI models — ours or anyone else's. Your data is used to generate your own outputs and nothing more. This applies to all plans, including Free.

Most tools show the host a consent option and call it done. Moon shows every participant the same indicator, at the same moment, with a one-tap opt-out that actually stops the recording for everyone — not just excludes that person's audio. It's the difference between disclosure and genuine consent.

Unlimited meetings — no monthly cap — with smart summaries and action items for each one. You get a 14-day searchable history and 3 highlight reels per month. The free tier is meant to be a real evaluation of Moon on your actual week, not a capped demo that forces a decision before you've seen it work.

Yes, with one important detail: Moon will still ask all participants for consent, and someone needs to allow it. If you're a guest in someone else's meeting, Moon will surface the consent prompt to everyone present, including the host. If anyone declines, Moon steps back — even if you invited it.

Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams are fully supported on all plans. Email summarization works with Gmail and Outlook. Slack integration is available on Team and above. If your stack is different, reach out — we track which platforms to prioritize next based on what teams actually ask for.
